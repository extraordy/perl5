Data Mapping with Hashes - Quiz 1

In what order is the result of the keys() function?

Unreliable, inconsistent, unordered, none. Any of these will work.

Long form answer: It's not an order that will make any sense to you, it isn't useful for anything, and it can change without warning.

The point that the hash won't be in a particular order can't be tested easily by students because it has to do with a number of factors as written about in perldoc -f keys, perldoc perlsec, and perldoc perlrun.

The ordering of a hash is only changed if it looks like there's a possible attack occurring and most hashes won't be accessed to the extent to actually show this.

In order for the student to see that the hash ordering is indeed random, you can direct them to use the PERL_HASH_SEED_DEBUG environment variable when running their program.

Grading Criteria: Student should demonstrate understanding of hashes and the underlying storage of information.
