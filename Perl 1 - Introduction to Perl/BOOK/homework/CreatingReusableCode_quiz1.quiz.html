Creating Reusable Code - Quiz 1

When naming a subroutine, how can you be sure that its name doesn't conflict with a Perl built-in?

Either do not name your subroutine the same as a built-in, or prepend your sub with a _ (underscore) to denote that it is a local copy.

Grading Criteria: Student should demonstrate an understanding of how Perl namespaces subroutines.

What happens if you create a subroutine with a name that conflicts with a built-in function?

Most often, Perl will ignore your sub and use the built-in, sometimes you will get a warning depending on how tightly coupled the sub is (for example, UNIVERSAL::* will often throw a warning, though we do not really cover this in the lesson).

Grading Criteria: Student should demonstrate ability to both read documentation and build a test script.
