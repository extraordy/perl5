Getting Started - Quiz 2

How would you limit the scope of $area to the minimum necessary visibility in the following code:

Observe:
my $PI = 3.14159265;
my $radius = 71.5;
my $area = $PI * $radius ** 2;
print "Area of circle of radius $radius is $area\n";
my $volume = 4/3 * $PI * $radius ** 3;
print "Volume of sphere of radius $radius is $volume\n";
my $height = 21;
$volume = $PI * $radius ** 2 * $height;
print "Volume of cylinder of radius $radius and height $height is $volume\n";

By the use of a naked block:

Observe:
my $PI = 3.14159265;
my $radius = 71.5;
{ my $area = $PI * $radius ** 2;
  print "Area of circle of radius $radius is $area\n";
}
my $volume = 4/3 * $PI * $radius ** 3;
print "Volume of sphere of radius $radius is $volume\n";
my $height = 21;
$volume = $PI * $radius ** 2 * $height;
print "Volume of cylinder of radius $radius and height $height is $volume\n";

Grading Criteria: Student should demonstrate knowledge of how to properly scope a variable.
